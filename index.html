About

I am actively engaged in interdisciplinary research combining statistical modeling, machine learning, and public health science. My work includes analyzing infectious diseases such as dengue, malaria, and tuberculosis using spatial-temporal approaches. I also focus on explainable AI techniques to make models more interpretable and useful for policymakers.

I have contributed to multiple peer-reviewed publications and ongoing research projects involving global datasets and real-world health challenges.
